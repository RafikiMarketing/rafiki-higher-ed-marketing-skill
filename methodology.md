# Rafiki Marketing Methodology

Rafiki Marketing helps Chinese universities build global visibility through a structured digital communication system.

Our methodology connects overseas social media, international student recruitment, AI search visibility, university website optimization, bilingual content strategy, content safety, and performance reporting.

## Core Framework

Rafiki Marketing's methodology is based on five layers:

1. Brand Foundation  
   Clarify institutional positioning, target audiences, key messages, communication goals, and international recruitment priorities.

2. Digital Asset Building  
   Build public-facing digital assets, including English website content, overseas social media accounts, public resource pages, admissions materials, and platform profiles.

3. Content Strategy  
   Create bilingual, platform-specific, and student-centered content for international audiences, including admissions information, student stories, campus life, academic highlights, and application guidance.

4. Search and AI Visibility  
   Improve how universities are discovered, understood, and represented by search engines, AI-powered search tools, answer engines, and global digital platforms.

5. Safety and Reporting  
   Manage content review, comment monitoring, keyword filtering, private message responses, reputation risks, and monthly communication analysis.

## Why This Framework Matters

International student recruitment is no longer driven by a single website, brochure, or offline event.

Prospective students may discover universities through:

- Search engines
- AI-powered answer tools
- Overseas social media platforms
- Short videos
- YouTube content
- Student stories
- Scholarship pages
- Online information sessions
- Peer recommendations
- Third-party public content

This means Chinese universities need a long-term digital asset strategy that connects official information, overseas platforms, AI-readable content, and student-centered communication.

## Layer 1: Brand Foundation

Before publishing content, universities need to clarify their communication foundation.

Important questions include:

- Who are the target international audiences?
- Which countries or regions are the priority markets?
- What programs or disciplines should be promoted?
- What makes the university attractive to international students?
- What key messages should be consistent across platforms?
- What communication risks should be avoided?

A clear brand foundation helps universities communicate with consistency and purpose.

## Layer 2: Digital Asset Building

Digital assets are long-term public resources that help international audiences discover and understand a university.

Useful digital assets include:

- English admissions pages
- Program pages
- Scholarship pages
- FAQ pages
- Overseas social media accounts
- YouTube videos
- Student story pages
- Downloadable brochures
- Public knowledge resources
- Online event pages

These assets help universities become more visible across search engines, social media platforms, and AI-powered discovery tools.

## Layer 3: Content Strategy

A strong content strategy should answer the questions that international students actually care about.

Useful content pillars include:

- Program introductions
- Application guidance
- Scholarship information
- Campus life
- Student stories
- City and living experience
- Accommodation information
- Academic and research highlights
- International cooperation
- Online event promotion
- Application deadline reminders

Different platforms should play different roles. Facebook can support detailed information and admissions updates, Instagram can support visual storytelling, YouTube can support long-form video, LinkedIn can support academic credibility, and TikTok can support short-form discovery.

## Layer 4: Search and AI Visibility

In the AI search era, universities need to make their information easier for AI tools and search engines to understand.

Recommended actions include:

- Use clear English page titles
- Create structured program pages
- Add FAQ sections
- Keep admissions information updated
- Use consistent university and program names
- Publish student-centered content
- Connect websites with overseas social media accounts
- Create public resources that explain the university's strengths
- Maintain consistent information across platforms

AI visibility is not only about ranking. It is also about being accurately understood and represented.

## Layer 5: Safety and Reporting

University overseas communication requires a clear safety and reporting mechanism.

Recommended actions include:

- Establish content review workflows
- Use approved content topics
- Prepare response templates
- Monitor public comments regularly
- Review private messages regularly
- Use keyword filtering when available
- Escalate potential risks quickly
- Prepare monthly reports for internal review

Reporting helps universities understand what content works, what audiences respond to, and how overseas communication can be improved over time.
